Become a host family

High School Host Families

Couples or families typically host a student for three months or more. The ideal host family is eager to share aspects of American life with the student while being open to learning about other cultures.

Host parents are interviewed by the committee and by law must complete a background check. Host parents that are selected are expected to sign a contract with the committee, promising to fulfill certain obligations, and are provided with a set of rules that govern the conduct and expectations of the students.

Junior High Host Families

amilies are needed each March for approximately one week to host a junior high student.

The ideal host family is excited to expose the student to typical American life by being warm and welcoming. During the week, host families will be responsible for picking up and dropping off their student for daily committee arranged activities, attending the welcome and farewell parties, and spending time with the student during the evenings and weekend.

Benefits of Being a Host Family

  • Interact with another culture
  • Share American culture, food, and customs
  • Create life-long friendships
  • Gain a global perspective
  • Have fun

Become a committee member

The Bloomington-Normal Asahikawa Sister Cities Committee is a volunteer committee that oversees all aspects of the sister city friendship. There are 20 board members with 10 members appointed by the City of Bloomington and 10 members appointed by the Town of Normal.

Member Commitments

Member Commitments

Committee members meet monthly and are expected to actively participate in at least one subcommittee:

  • High School Exchange to Asahikawa
  • High School Exchange from Asahikawa
  • Junior Ambassadors from Asahikawa
  • Junior Ambassadors to Asahikawa

These subcommittees facilitate the work of the committee on a weekly basis.
